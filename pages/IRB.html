Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB)

The Immigration and Refugee Board (IRB) is Canadaâ€™s largest administrative tribunal. The IRB is responsible for conducting hearings on refugee matters, immigration appeals such as sponsorship and residency obligation appeals as well as Inadmissibility hearings for misrepresentation.

Refugees

Refugees travel far and wide fleeing persecution and oppression to discover a safe haven in Canada. Currently, Canada has a two-tier refugee system with countries that are deemed low risk (Designated Countries of Origin) and countries that are deemed high risk because of war, political and social oppression as well as religious strife. Refugees coming from DCO states are processed faster through the IRB and typically have their hearings in a much shorter time span. Refugees coming from war-torn territories have a much lengthier process and have a higher level of success.

If a refugee claim is refused, the claim can be referred to the Refugee Appeal Division (RAD) for a subsequent hearing and a reconsideration of the facts. The likelihood of success is premised on the strength of the case and the nature of the evidence before the Board.

Immigration Appeal Division (IAD)

The Immigration Appeal Division (IAD) deals with spousal and residency obligation appeals. If a spousal application is refused by a visa office abroad, the sponsor can file a Notice of Appeal with the IAD to have the matter reconsidered before a Panel Member. Residency Obligation appeals are a judicial process as a result of a permanent resident who has failed to meet the requirements of their residency obligation in accordance with the Immigration and Refugee Protection Act.

Detention Reviews and Admissibility Hearings

The IRB also conducts hearings for detention reviews and admissibility hearings. There are many reasons that a person may be detained and/or found inadmissible. If this is the case, it is very important that you seek legal representation immediately to avoid any further immigration repercussions.
