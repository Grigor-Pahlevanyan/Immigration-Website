Adamant Immigration Consulting

“Adamant” Immigration Consulting facilitates the transfer of workers between foreign and Canadian companies whether they come to work on temporary assignments or to make a business visit. Canada has many immigration programs designed to help executives, senior managers, intra-company transferees with specialized knowledge, as well as business owners and entrepreneurs to come and work in Canada and bring their families along with them.

LMIA-exempt work permits

Work permits obtained pursuant to Foreign Trade Agreements (NAFTA, CETA, and CCFTA).

Depending on the FTA, there is a list of approximately 60 occupations that qualify for a facilitated work permit for professionals. To be eligible for a professional work permit under one of the above agreements, the individual usually must either have a diploma or work experience in the occupation or both (depending on the field of specialization).

Work Without a Work Permit

Foreign nationals may perform work in Canada without a work permit if they are one of the following:

  • Business Visitors
  • Foreign Representatives and their Family Members
  • Military Personnel
  • Foreign Government Officers
  • American Cross-Border Maritime Law Enforcement Officers
  • In-Flight Security Officers
  • On-Campus Employment
  • Performing Artists
  • Athletes and Team Members
  • News Reporters, Media Crews
  • Public Speakers
  • Convention Organizers
  • Clergy
  • Judges, Referees, and Similar Officials
  • Examiners and Evaluators
  • Expert Witnesses and Investigators
  • Health Care Students
  • Civil Aviation Inspectors
  • Aviation or Accident Investigators
  • Crew
  • Emergency Service Providers
  • Implied Status

Please note that an individual who is eligible to work without a work permit may still require a Temporary Resident Visa to enter Canada depending on his country of citizenship.
